The process of how participants (college students) organize and plan grocery shopping in their daily life.

- Convenience is an important factor in grocery decisions.
- Price awareness may decrease over time once students become familiar with local prices.
- Personal preference strongly influences store choice.
- Participant prioritizes fresh food on a daily basis rather than bulk purchases.
- Grocery shopping behavior is integrated into daily routines instead of planned in advance.

=== Page 21 ===
Post-Session Reflection

Top 3 Insights from this Session

1. Food quality (freshness and taste) is the most important factor influencing the participant's grocery decisions.

2. Price still matters, but it is less critical because the participant has financial support from her family as an international student.

3. The planning phase of grocery shopping is relatively short.  
   Many decisions occur during the shopping trip itself rather than beforehand.


Biggest Surprise

The participant purchases groceries in between daily activities not primarily to save time but to ensure that the food she buys is fresh.


Questions for Follow-up / Things to Explore

Q: Would you prefer shopping at Fresh Market or at a store closer to your home?

A: The participant says she would still choose Fresh Market because it is more convenient to stop there between daily activities and the route fits naturally into her schedule.

Q: Is there a time when you seriously consider food prices when buying groceries?

A: When she first arrived in the United States she compared prices frequently with those in her home country.  
Now that she is familiar with local prices she does not focus as heavily on price unless it is unusually high.


How this Connects to Our Focus Objectives

College students may care strongly about food quality and freshness.  
A grocery-planning application could potentially include information about store restocking times so users know when the freshest products are available.


Team Debrief Notes

- International students may prioritize food quality more than price if they receive financial support from family.
- Travel time to grocery stores is an important factor influencing store choice and should be considered in design solutions.
